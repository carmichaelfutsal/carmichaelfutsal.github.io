  1. Each team has a goalkeeper and 4 players on the field (5 total)- minimum 3
  2. The Carmichael Futsal League will be played on a basketball court
  3. The penalty box is a semi circle with a ~4m radius
  4. No off-sides
  5. When the entire ball goes out of bounds over the sideline, play is restarted with a kick-in
  6. The kick-in cannot go directly into the goal, except corner kicks
  7. When the ball goes out over the end line, it is put back into play either by the goalie throwing the ball back into the playing area, or awarding a Corner Kick
  8. After a goal has been scored, it is put back into play by a kick-off
  9. Home team opens with kickoff, away team opens 2nd half with kickoff
  10. Players can score from anywhere on the field
  11. Scoring- when the entire ball passes over the goal line, between the goal posts and under the crossbar (except by illegal means)
  12. All fouls result in direct kicks
  13. If, while the ball is still in play, the referees are required to stop play temporarily for any reason not mentioned elsewhere in these rules, the match is restarted with a kickin by the team that posessed the ball or a drop ball if posession was not established
  14. Each game is 34 minutes in duration (2x 17 minute halves, running clock only stops for 5 minutes at halftime or for prolonged injuries)
  15. Equipment: shorts, socks, shin-guards and footwear with flat, rubber soles
  16. Ball sizes:
    • U12 and under - size 3 or a Select "Jinga"
    • U14 and older – size 4 FUTSAL ball

Referee

Referees manage the game to ensure a safe environment and that players put the ball back into play properly. Referees dress distinctly from players.

Timekeeper

Start game clock after kick-off, indicate end of first half and match with some sort of sound; record scorers, and players cautioned and sent off, and other information relevant to the game.

Kick-in

  • To be taken in place of the throw-in, placed on touchline before kicking.
  • The kick-in must be taken within 5 seconds; if it is not, the kick-in is given to the opposing team.
  • The kicker cannot play the ball a second time until it has been played by another player.
  • Players on opposing team must be at least 3 m away from point of kick-in.
  • Cannot score directly from a kick-in.

Goal Clearance

  • To be taken in place of goal kick.
  • From inside the penalty area, the goalkeeper throws the ball into play.
  • The ball is not in play until it has passed outside of the penalty area. If the goal clearance is received inside of the penalty area, the goal clearance shall be taken over.

Corner Kick

  • Ball placed on the corner (no corner-kick arc). If ball is misplaced, the corner kick is re-taken.
  • Must be taken within 5 seconds or turned over to other team
  • The kicker cannot play the ball a second time until it has been played by another player.
  • Opposing players must be at least 3m away from point of the corner kick.
  • Can score goal directly from a corner kick.

Fouls and Misconduct

All fouls are penalized with a direct free kick for the following offences: handball (except goalkeeper within penalty area), kick, trip, charge, push, hold, slide tackle, spit or attempt to do so. A direct free kick is taken from the place where the offence occurred unless the foul is committed by a player inside his own penalty area, in which case a ‘penalty kick’ is awarded- from the penalty spot.

  • Opposing players must be at least 3 meters away until the ball is in play
  • Ball is in play after it has rolled at least once
  • Kick must be taken within 5 seconds
  • Kicker cannot touch the ball again until it has been touched by another player

Penalty Kick

A penalty kick is awarded against a team that commits a foul inside its own penalty area.

  • To be taken from the penalty mark on the midpoint of the 6-m-line.
  • All players must be out of the penalty area, opposing team must be at least 3 m away.
  • The kicker shall not play the ball a second time until it has been touched by another player.
  • The goalkeeper shall remain in his penalty area starting within 1 m of goal line.

Yellow Card Offenses:

Can be given for any of the fouls above- if committed to a greater/reckless degree or if the foul is intentional. Two yellow cards in the same match will result in a red card.

Red Card Offenses:

Carmichael Futsal has a strict policy for red card violations. Any player issued a red card will become ineligible from finishing the current match and is suspended from the next scheduled match. The player, parent, and coach will be required to meet with the league manager prior to their next eligible game. Red Card violations include: malicious intent or unsportsmanlike conduct. The player sent off (shown a red card) is out for the rest of the game and cannot sit on the bench.
